## 1. Target (exact current statement)

The project's single non-legacy sorry, `InfinitaryLogic/Conditional/GandyHarrington.lean`:

```lean
theorem gandy_harrington_for_relation {α : Type u}
    [MetricSpace α] [CompleteSpace α] [SecondCountableTopology α]
    [MeasurableSpace α] [BorelSpace α]
    (r : Setoid α) (hr : MeasurableSet {p : α × α | r.r p.1 p.2})
    (hunc : ¬ Countable (Quotient r)) :
    ∃ f : (ℕ → Bool) → α,
      Continuous f ∧ Function.Injective f ∧
      ∀ a b, a ≠ b → ¬ r.r (f a) (f b)
```

## 2. Route audit and decision

Two candidate routes were compared, as anticipated:

**Route 1 — classical category (Miller).** Source: B. D. Miller, *The graph-theoretic
approach to descriptive set theory*, BSL 18(4) 2012, Theorem 11 (Silver) and Theorem 8
(Kechris–Solecki–Todorcevic `G₀`-dichotomy, with classical proof sketch on p. 6); expanded in
Miller's *Forceless, ineffective, powerless proofs of descriptive dichotomy theorems*
(Lecture I: Silver's theorem). The proof of Silver from `G₀`:

1. Apply the `G₀`-dichotomy to the graph `G = ¬E` off the diagonal. A Borel ℵ₀-coloring of
   `G` means countably many classes (each color class lies in one `E`-class). Otherwise there
   is a dense `S ⊆ 2^<ℕ` and a continuous homomorphism `φ : 2^ℕ → X` from `G_S(2^ℕ)` to `¬E`.
2. The pullback `E' = (φ × φ)⁻¹(E)` is a Borel equivalence relation on `2^ℕ` whose classes
   are `G_S(2^ℕ)`-independent, hence meager (Miller Prop. 6: for dense `S`, a non-meager set
   with the Baire property is not `G_S`-independent — proved by the coordinate-flip
   homeomorphism).
3. **Kuratowski–Ulam**: all sections of the Baire-property set `E'` meager ⟹ `E'` meager.
4. **Mycielski** (CDST 19.1): `E'` meager ⟹ continuous `ψ : 2^ℕ → 2^ℕ` with distinct points
   `E'`-inequivalent.
5. `f = φ ∘ ψ` is the perfect antichain (injectivity from the antichain property +
   reflexivity).

Everything is classical: no forcing, no effective DST, no recursion-theoretic analysis.
The classical proof of the `G₀`-dichotomy itself (the hard core) uses the σ-ideal `I_G`
generated by `G`-independent Borel sets and, for each `n`, the ideal `I_n` on
`Hom(G_S(2^n), G)` defined by `Φ ∈ I_n ⟺ ∃ Borel B ∈ I_G, ∀ φ ∈ Φ, ∃ s ∈ 2^n, φ(s) ∈ B`;
the key step is that combining pairs of homomorphisms sends `I_n`-positive sets to
`I_{n+1}`-positive sets, giving either a transfinite Cantor–Bendixson-style argument or a
one-step condensation-style argument. Lusin's first separation theorem (already in mathlib
as `MeasureTheory.AnalyticSet.measurablySeparable`) is used to extract Borel independent sets.

**Route 2 — lightface Gandy–Harrington (Harrington).** Conditions are nonempty Σ¹₁ sets in
the GH topology; fusion via strong Choquet. Requires effective DST from scratch: recursive
presentations, ℕ-coded Borel/analytic syntax (W-type or well-founded codes — naive
`ℕ → BorelCode` is uncountable), coded sections (~1000+ lines before the theorem starts; see
the prior assessment in project memory).

**Decision: Route 1.** Rationale:
- All four ingredients are classical and bounded; two (Kuratowski–Ulam, Mycielski) are
  generic mathlib gap-fillers of independent value.
- Mathlib already has the meager/residual/Baire-property API the route consumes (§4).
- Route 2's prerequisite layer is an unbounded research project.

**Key negative finding (why no `GHCondition` forcing layer on `α`).** The originally sketched
interface — `GHCondition` with `carrier`/`split`/`fusion` on subsets of `α` feeding
`CantorScheme.exists_antichain_map` — does **not** fit Route 1: the forcing-style largeness in
Miller's `G₀` proof lives on sets of *finite partial homomorphisms* `2^n → X`, not subsets of
`α`, and the final antichain is the composite `φ ∘ ψ`, not the limit of a scheme on `α`.
Finite-stage sibling cross-avoidance (the `hcross` of `exists_antichain_map`) is impossible
for dense relations such as the pullback of `E₀`: any two sets with nonempty interior meet a
dense relation, so the Mycielski step necessarily achieves avoidance only in the limit, with
constraints scheduled across levels. `CantorScheme.exists_antichain_map` therefore stays as
the assembly point for the *closed* case (`silver_core_closed`) and for a possible future
Route 2, but is not on the Route 1 path. The Route 1 interface layer is instead the two
hypothesis Props below.

## 3. Chosen interface layer (scaffolded, assembly proved)

`InfinitaryLogic/Conditional/SilverCategoryRoute.lean`:

- `MycielskiCantorHypothesis : Prop` — meager equivalence relation on `ℕ → Bool` admits a
  continuous map with distinct points inequivalent (steps 4).
- `CategoryReductionHypothesis : Prop` — Borel relation with uncountable quotient admits a
  continuous `φ : 2^ℕ → α` with meager pullback (steps 1–3 packaged at the exact altitude
  consumed downstream).
- `gandy_harrington_of_category_route` — **proved, axiom-clean**
  (`[propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound]`): the two hypotheses imply the exact statement
  of `gandy_harrington_for_relation` (step 5).

The deliberately coarse granularity of `CategoryReductionHypothesis` avoids freezing
interfaces for `G_S` graphs / Kuratowski–Ulam / `G₀` before work on them starts — the audit's
lesson is that premature interface-freezing is the main design risk.

  **Remaining construction blueprint** (worked out 2026-06-10; sub-blocks 3–4):

  *Setup.* `G := {p | ¬ r.r p.1 p.2}` is Borel hence analytic; `G` nonempty (else one
  class), so by mathlib's `AnalyticSet` definition `G = range g` for a continuous
  `g : (ℕ → ℕ) → α × α`. Suslin witnesses for edges are exactly `g`-preimages; no separate
  tree representation is needed. Vertices at level `n` are `Fin n → Bool`; the active edges
  at level `n` are pairs `(u, v)` agreeing with `canonicalWord m` below `L_m`, differing at
  `L_m`, equal above (for `L_m < n`) — these are precisely the level-`n` prefixes of
  `GSGraph canonicalS` edge pairs, and each level-`(n+1)` edge is a `snoc` of a level-`n`
  edge or the fresh cross pair at `s = canonicalWord m` when `L_m = n`.

  *Stage data* (recursion over levels): an analytic positive (`¬ SmallFam`) family
  `Φ_n ⊆ (Fin n → Bool) → α`, vertex controls `V_u` (basic opens of diameter `≤ 2⁻ⁿ` with
  `∀ φ ∈ Φ_n, φ u ∈ V_u`, and children's values lying in parent `V`s), and per-active-edge
  witness words `c_e : List ℕ` of length `≥ n - birth(e)` with
  `∀ φ ∈ Φ_n, ∃ w ∈ cylinder c_e, g w = (φ u, φ v)`.

  *Combination lemma* (sub-block 3 core): `comb(Φ) := {snoc-combine φ₀ φ₁ | φ₀, φ₁ ∈ Φ,`
  cross pair in `G` (when a cross word exists)`}` preserves positivity: if a Borel capture
  `B` worked for `comb(Φ)`, then `Φ' := Φ ∩ {φ | ∀ u, φ u ∉ B}` is positive and analytic,
  and no two of its members combine, so `eval_s(Φ')` is `G`-independent and analytic; the
  KST superset lemma gives a Borel independent capture of `Φ'` — contradiction. (With no
  cross word, `combine φ φ` already contradicts the capture.) Analyticity of `comb(Φ)`
  needs `AnalyticSet.prod` (build via `(ℕ → ℕ) × (ℕ → ℕ) ≃ₜ (ℕ → ℕ)` if missing from
  mathlib) and the witnessed-cross-pair set as a closed-set projection.

  *Per-level refinement folds*: vertex shrinking (cover `α` by balls of radius `2⁻ⁿ`
  around a countable dense set; `Φ = ⋃` over assignments of basic opens — countable, so
  the pigeonhole keeps a positive piece) and witness extension (`Φ = ⋃` over one-step
  extensions of `c_e` — countable). Finitely many constraints per level, folded
  sequentially as in the Mycielski/KU pair-killing folds.

  *Fusion* (sub-block 4): pick `φ_n ∈ Φ_n` (positive ⟹ nonempty); `a_n(x) := φ_n(x|n)`
  is Cauchy (consecutive values share the level-`n` vertex control), giving
  `φ(x) := lim a_n`, continuous since `x|n` determines `φ x` to within `2^{1-n}`. For a
  `GSGraph` edge `(y, z)` born of `canonicalWord m`: the witness words `c_e^n` along the
  branch pair nest and grow, defining `w ∈ ℕ → ℕ`; stage witnesses `w_n → w`, so by
  continuity `g w = lim g w_n = lim (φ_n u_n, φ_n v_n) = (φ y, φ z) ∈ G`. This yields
  `GSGraphHomHypothesis`, and `gandy_harrington_of_gSGraphHom` fells the last sorry.

Do not modify `CantorAntichain.lean`, `silver_core_closed`, or the statement of
`gandy_harrington_for_relation` in any of these phases.

## 2. Route audit and decision

Two candidate routes were compared, as anticipated:

**Route 1 ‚ÄĒ classical category (Miller).** Source: B. D. Miller, *The graph-theoretic
approach to descriptive set theory*, BSL 18(4) 2012, Theorem 11 (Silver) and Theorem 8
(Kechris‚ÄďSolecki‚ÄďTodorcevic `G‚āÄ`-dichotomy, with classical proof sketch on p. 6); expanded in
Miller's *Forceless, ineffective, powerless proofs of descriptive dichotomy theorems*
(Lecture I: Silver's theorem). The proof of Silver from `G‚āÄ`:

1. Apply the `G‚āÄ`-dichotomy to the graph `G = ¬¨E` off the diagonal. A Borel ‚ĄĶ‚āÄ-coloring of
   `G` means countably many classes (each color class lies in one `E`-class). Otherwise there
   is a dense `S ‚äÜ 2^<‚Ąē` and a continuous homomorphism `ŌÜ : 2^‚Ąē ‚Üí X` from `G_S(2^‚Ąē)` to `¬¨E`.
2. The pullback `E' = (ŌÜ √ó ŌÜ)‚ĀĽ¬Ļ(E)` is a Borel equivalence relation on `2^‚Ąē` whose classes
   are `G_S(2^‚Ąē)`-independent, hence meager (Miller Prop. 6: for dense `S`, a non-meager set
   with the Baire property is not `G_S`-independent ‚ÄĒ proved by the coordinate-flip
   homeomorphism).
3. **Kuratowski‚ÄďUlam**: all sections of the Baire-property set `E'` meager ‚üĻ `E'` meager.
4. **Mycielski** (CDST 19.1): `E'` meager ‚üĻ continuous `Ōą : 2^‚Ąē ‚Üí 2^‚Ąē` with distinct points
   `E'`-inequivalent.
5. `f = ŌÜ ‚ąė Ōą` is the perfect antichain (injectivity from the antichain property +
   reflexivity).

Everything is classical: no forcing, no effective DST, no recursion-theoretic analysis.
The classical proof of the `G‚āÄ`-dichotomy itself (the hard core) uses the ŌÉ-ideal `I_G`
generated by `G`-independent Borel sets and, for each `n`, the ideal `I_n` on
`Hom(G_S(2^n), G)` defined by `ő¶ ‚ąą I_n ‚üļ ‚ąÉ Borel B ‚ąą I_G, ‚ąÄ ŌÜ ‚ąą ő¶, ‚ąÉ s ‚ąą 2^n, ŌÜ(s) ‚ąą B`;
the key step is that combining pairs of homomorphisms sends `I_n`-positive sets to
`I_{n+1}`-positive sets, giving either a transfinite Cantor‚ÄďBendixson-style argument or a
one-step condensation-style argument. Lusin's first separation theorem (already in mathlib
as `MeasureTheory.AnalyticSet.measurablySeparable`) is used to extract Borel independent sets.

**Route 2 ‚ÄĒ lightface Gandy‚ÄďHarrington (Harrington).** Conditions are nonempty ő£¬Ļ‚āĀ sets in
the GH topology; fusion via strong Choquet. Requires effective DST from scratch: recursive
presentations, ‚Ąē-coded Borel/analytic syntax (W-type or well-founded codes ‚ÄĒ naive
`‚Ąē ‚Üí BorelCode` is uncountable), coded sections (~1000+ lines before the theorem starts; see
the prior assessment in project memory).

**Decision: Route 1.** Rationale:
- All four ingredients are classical and bounded; two (Kuratowski‚ÄďUlam, Mycielski) are
  generic mathlib gap-fillers of independent value.
- Mathlib already has the meager/residual/Baire-property API the route consumes (¬ß4).
- Route 2's prerequisite layer is an unbounded research project.

**Key negative finding (why no `GHCondition` forcing layer on `őĪ`).** The originally sketched
interface ‚ÄĒ `GHCondition` with `carrier`/`split`/`fusion` on subsets of `őĪ` feeding
`CantorScheme.exists_antichain_map` ‚ÄĒ does **not** fit Route 1: the forcing-style largeness in
Miller's `G‚āÄ` proof lives on sets of *finite partial homomorphisms* `2^n ‚Üí X`, not subsets of
`őĪ`, and the final antichain is the composite `ŌÜ ‚ąė Ōą`, not the limit of a scheme on `őĪ`.
Finite-stage sibling cross-avoidance (the `hcross` of `exists_antichain_map`) is impossible
for dense relations such as the pullback of `E‚āÄ`: any two sets with nonempty interior meet a
dense relation, so the Mycielski step necessarily achieves avoidance only in the limit, with
constraints scheduled across levels. `CantorScheme.exists_antichain_map` therefore stays as
the assembly point for the *closed* case (`silver_core_closed`) and for a possible future
Route 2, but is not on the Route 1 path. The Route 1 interface layer is instead the two
hypothesis Props below.
